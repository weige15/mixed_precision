# FP8-LM: Training FP8 Large Language Models

- **Authors:** Houwen Peng, Kan Wu, Yixuan Wei, Guoshuai Zhao, Yuxiang Yang, Ze Liu, Yifan Xiong, Ziyue Yang, Bolin Ni, Jingcheng Hu, Ruihang Li, Miaosen Zhang, Chen Li, Jia Ning, Ruizhe Wang, Zheng Zhang, Shuguang Liu, Joe Chau, Han Hu, Peng Cheng
- **Year:** 2023
- **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.18313
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2310.18313

## Summary

FP8-LM proposes an automatic mixed-precision framework for LLM training. It incrementally applies FP8 to gradients, optimizer states, and distributed training components. The paper reports large memory and speed gains compared with BF16 Megatron-LM on H100 hardware, including a reported 39% real memory reduction and 75% faster GPT-175B training.

Importantly, the authors argue that many variables in LLM training can use lower precision without accuracy loss or hyperparameter changes. They also state that the methodology can apply beyond pretraining to instruction tuning and RLHF.

## Relevance To This Project

This paper motivates the broader research direction: if FP8 can be used across many training variables, then small controlled experiments should ask which components actually require high precision. H1 is a limited-resource probe of one such component: normalization during LoRA fine-tuning.

## Key Takeaways

- Mixed precision can include gradients and optimizer states, not just forward activations.
- The most useful policies are incremental and role-specific.
- The paper explicitly connects FP8 mixed precision to fine-tuning settings, though its headline evidence is large-scale.

## Evidence Gaps For H1

- The reported gains depend on H100-scale infrastructure and an FP8 framework, not ordinary PyTorch BF16 autocast.
- It does not tell us whether fp32 RMSNorm helps or is redundant during small LoRA fine-tuning.
