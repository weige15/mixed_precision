| 10 | 2026-05-14 | dtype-validity | Added and ran an H5 RMSNorm internal dtype probe. Combined with the earlier CUDA boundary dtype probe, this suggests Qwen RMSNorm reductions are already fp32 internally under baseline bf16 boundaries, weakening H1's original mechanistic rationale. |

| 12 | 2026-05-14 | results | Analyzed H6 Stage 1 bf16 calibration for seeds 42, 43, and 44. Each run observed 218 modules with zero NaN/Inf events, peak CUDA memory 2.7303 GiB, and highly stable decisions: 217/218 modules had identical assignments across seeds. The only unstable int8 candidate was `layers.23.mlp.gate_proj`, which crossed the threshold only for seed 42. The conservative policy leaves all attention projections bf16, promotes all norms/logits to fp32, and leaves nearly all MLP projections bf16. Stage 1 is therefore stable enough for perturbation testing but too conservative to freeze a resource-saving policy. |
| 13 | 2026-05-15 | results | Analyzed the first H6 Stage 2 perturbation probe on seed 42. Fake int8 output perturbation produced large loss increases for the three Stage-1 high-risk MLP down projections: +0.2867 for `layers.21.mlp.down_proj`, +0.2228 for `layers.2.mlp.down_proj`, and +0.1491 for `layers.3.mlp.down_proj`. The four relaxed low-risk late-layer MLP gate/up candidates had near-zero deltas between about -0.0042 and +0.0070. For MLP projections, Stage 1 max outlier score correlates with absolute perturbation loss delta at about 0.91, and max int8 relative MSE at about 0.81. This is positive evidence that cheap calibration signals predict projection sensitivity; replicate on seeds 43 and 44 before freezing a policy. |
| 14 | 2026-05-15 | results | Replicated H6 Stage 2 perturbation probes on seeds 43 and 44 and synthesized all three seeds. Across 21 MLP perturbations, max outlier score correlates with absolute loss delta at about 0.91. Next step: freeze a narrow H6 policy around `layers.22/23.mlp.gate_proj` and `layers.22/23.mlp.up_proj`, then run a short training comparison against bf16. |
| 15 | 2026-05-15 | results | Ran the first frozen H6 narrow-policy 500-step training comparison against the matched bf16 seed-42 control. The H6 fake-int8 late-layer MLP policy reached final eval loss 1.63112 versus bf16 1.62949, a +0.00163 degradation or about +0.10%, inside the locked 1% quality gate. Both runs had zero loss spikes and zero NaN/Inf events, and max grad norm was unchanged. Peak memory was essentially unchanged, while train throughput fell by about 13.5% because the current implementation uses Python-level fake-quant hooks. Conclusion: H6 has positive evidence for calibration-guided quality-preserving module selection, but no resource-saving claim yet. |
